Thesis: Evaluating interaction of underground excavations and groundwater resources based on experiences gained from long water conveyance tunneling projects in Iran

Advisers: Dr. J. Hassanpour and Dr. A. Cheshomi

First, in this study, a comprehensive database which contained information on 225 springs located in the vicinity of tunneling projects in Iran was developed. By analyzing the DHI method on this database, all aspects of impacts of tunneling on spring drawdown was studied. By learning from developed methods’ shortcomings, a new index was proposed. This new empirical classification system, named TIS (Tunneling Impacts on Spring), is based on statistical analysis of the developed database. This index is a part of a more practical approach. Using a screening process, the number of springs significantly reduces and only springs with possible influence from tunneling are consider for further assessments. This helps to investigate the in-risk springs thoroughly and design means to prevent them from happening.
