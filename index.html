About Me

My name is Ashkan Lotfipoor. I’m an Civil Engineering Ph.D. student at the Heriot-Watt University and my research interest is to combine my knowledge of data science and engineering to solve complex engineering problems by mining and modeling the data streams from different sources.

My current research will develop an ambitious, genuinely novel and potentially transformative hybrid system of data-centric approaches that can provide academic/industry stakeholders with unprecedented capabilities for analysing, simulating and forecasting demand. This will be achieved by developing a data-driven modelling schematic that will utilise high resolution energy demand data, at a considerably fine temporal (5 or less minutes).

Also, for my master thesis, I used AHP method to develop a new mean for estimating the impacts of tunneling on springs discharge rate. At the same time, I worked at Samsung Field Marketing as a Data Analyst. During this period, I found a new appreciation for using data to solve complex problems and for this reason, I’m eager to learn more in this path and to use the techniques that I’ve learned to uncover new grounds in science.
